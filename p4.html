Exercise 4 -- Digital Tesslation and Mod

For Exercise 4, we used Illustrator to create Digital Tesslation. First, we decided to create this piece with a congruent triangle with a side length of 10cm. The two sides of the triangle were designed by each of us independently. The other side is a shared side for the team. One team member was responsible for designing and sharing the design with each team member. Finally, another group member and I were responsible for putting our team's Digital Tesslation into random combinations. As shown in the picture.

Goldilocks tests

After considering different notch sizes, our group decided to use a notch fastener for our project. Specifically, we chose a notch that is wider on the outside and narrower on the inside, as it securely locks the two parts together. The depth is uniformly one inch.
